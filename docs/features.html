Features

Everything Percolate does, in one place.

The Pick

What should I brew today?

Tell Percolate what you’re in the mood for, bright and fruity, rich and chocolatey, an easy daily cup, and it suggests a coffee from the bags you already own, matched on roast, origin, process, and flavor.

Discovery is fine, but the everyday question is “what do I brew right now?”, and the answer should come from your shelf, not a shopping cart.

Shelf

Your coffee, perfectly organized

Every bag in one place. Track each coffee by roaster, origin, and status, owned, wishlist, or finished. Roast level, process, and elevation are kept per bag so the shelf reads like a cupping table.

Filter by origin, process, or roast. Sort by what you’ve brewed least recently, so the back of the shelf gets its turn before the beans go stale.

Taste Profile

A flavor quiz that learns

Answer a few questions about how you like your coffee, roast, acidity, body, and the flavors you reach for, and Percolate starts matching you to beans you’ll actually enjoy.

Your palate sharpens with every cupping note you log. Built from your real shelf and your real cups, not from a single five-question quiz.

Browse

Find your next bag

Browse single origins, blends, and espresso roasts by roaster, origin, process, or flavor. Curated by how the coffee actually tastes, not by what a store wants to move this week.

Every coffee links out to its roaster so you can buy when you’re ready. No shopping pressure, no algorithmic push, just a well-organized catalog with affiliate disclosure on the page.

Bean Detail & Scan

Every coffee, scanned and remembered

Point your camera at a bag and Percolate reads the label to pull the roaster, origin, process, and roast, no typing out a long washed Ethiopian by hand. Tap any coffee to see its full profile, community rating, and your own cupping history.

When you’re ready to buy, “Buy from” links connect you directly to the roaster, with affiliate disclosure right on the page.

My Palate

See your taste take shape

Every cup you log feeds My Palate, your profile across roast, acidity, body, and the origins and processes you keep coming back to. The washed Colombians you reach for daily and the natural you rated once both reveal themselves.

At year-end, a Year in Coffee recap sums up your top roasters, origins, total cups, and the most-brewed bag of the year. Sharable as an image card.
